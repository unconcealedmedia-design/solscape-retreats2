How It Started

Built by women who live it

SolScape started because Nada and Samantha couldn't find what they were looking for. They'd tried the yoga holidays that were really just holidays. The wellness retreats that were really just spas. The group travel that felt like herding strangers through airports with a loosely planned schedule and mediocre food.

They wanted something with structure — real movement, real practice, real effort — inside a setting beautiful enough to make the body remember why it wants to move. Small enough to feel personal. Intentional enough to actually work.

So they built it. Between them: certification in barre instruction, a decade of yoga practice, years of teaching people how to use their bodies and quiet their minds, and a shared conviction that travel should leave you more yourself than when you left.

SolScape began in Spain, moved through Tuscany, and is now in Croatia — and it is still, fundamentally, what they set out to build: the retreat they would both want to go on.

The Founders

Meet Nada & Samantha

Nada

Barre Instructor & Co-Founder

Nada has spent years studying how the body moves — and why it stops. A certified barre instructor, she builds every physical session around precision and progression, not performance. Her classes are structured and demanding, done with enough care that you don't notice either of those things until after.

She has led sessions for athletes, beginners, people recovering from injury, and people who haven't moved properly in years. She believes there is a version of physical practice available to every body — and she spends her teaching life finding it for each person individually.

@solscape_retreats

Samantha

Yoga Teacher & Co-Founder

Samantha's relationship with yoga started long before SolScape. For her it was never about the poses — it was about finding something steady when everything else wasn't. That's what comes through in every session she leads: a practice that's honest, unhurried, and built around what you actually need rather than what looks good.

She holds a 500-hour yoga teacher certification and has studied breathwork, meditation, and somatic practices for over eight years. She leads the morning sessions, the breathwork circles, and the sound baths that end each evening.

@solscape_retreats

What We Stand For

Three things we'll never compromise

Movement

Every retreat is built around real, structured physical practice. Not token yoga at sunrise and the rest of the day on a sunbed. We train. We breathe. We sweat. We rest properly because we've earned it.

Community

Small groups aren't a marketing decision — they're what makes SolScape work. You can't build anything real in a group of thirty strangers. Our retreats create conditions for connection, not the performance of it.

Sustainability

We work exclusively with properties that take environmental responsibility seriously. We keep groups small so footprints stay light. And we donate 2% of every booking to local reforestation and conservation.
