Overview

Where movement meets art

Season one of our Catalonia retreat took a small group of women through Figueres and the surrounding landscape — a region that somehow manages to combine wild natural beauty with one of the most concentrated pockets of surrealist art on the planet.

Each day balanced structured movement in the morning with culture and exploration in the afternoon. A private visit to the Dalí Theatre-Museum. Coastal drives to hidden coves. Long evenings on the terrace with natural wines from the Empordà region, talking about what moves us.

Where You'll Stay

A farmhouse in the Empordà

We stayed in a restored 19th-century farmhouse in the Alt Empordà — a working landscape of vineyards, cork oaks, and low stone walls just twenty minutes from the coast. The house has the kind of quiet that is increasingly rare: deep, unhurried, and slightly golden at all hours.

Rooms are spacious with exposed stone walls, local ceramics, and linen everything. Morning sessions happen in the courtyard as the light comes in sideways through the olive trees. The pool is cold and perfect. The evenings are long.
